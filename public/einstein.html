Albert Einstein

Theoretical Physicist & Thought Leader

Albert Einstein (1879-1955) revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and energy. His theory of relativity showed that mass and energy are equivalent, and that gravity is a curvature of spacetime itself—not a force.

Einstein's work demonstrated that the universe operates on mathematical principles that can be observed, measured, and predicted. His approach to physics emphasized thought experiments, empirical validation, and the rejection of assumptions that cannot be proven.

This trading algorithm embodies Einstein's methodology: it observes market forces, calculates probabilities using multi-dimensional data, and makes decisions based on measurable patterns rather than emotion or speculation. Every trade is treated as a physics experiment—rigorous, repeatable, and subject to the laws of probability.
